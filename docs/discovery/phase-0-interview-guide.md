# Phase 0 Interview Guide

## Purpose

Use the same shared product backbone to test two wedges without mixing the story:

- self-coached solo-first
- coach clipboard (assign, see completion, tiny outcome signal, adjust next)

Each interview should test a believable workflow, not generic interest.

## Moderator rules

- Ask about current behavior before showing the product idea.
- Keep the wedge framing clean. Do not switch personas halfway through.
- Test a realistic session scenario, not abstract feature opinions.
- Record what was human-assisted versus what the product concept provided.
- End with a concrete repeat-use and payment question.

## Shared opener

Use for both wedges:

1. Tell me about the last time you tried to plan or run a beach training session.
2. What made that annoying, slow, or unreliable?
3. What tools or workarounds did you use?

## Wedge A: Self-Coached Solo-First

### Best participant profile

- amateur beach player
- trains 1-3 times per week
- often lacks a coach
- sometimes trains alone

### Core scenario

"You want to improve passing fundamentals for serve receive, and right now you have about 10-15 minutes, one ball, and maybe no partner. The app gives you a short starter session first, then can gather more detail later."

### Questions

1. How often do you train alone versus with another person?
2. If you had 10-15 minutes today, what would you actually do without help?
3. Where do you currently get your drills or structure from?
4. When you train alone, what do you actually have access to: wall, fence, net, open sand, extra balls, markers?
5. What makes you trust or not trust training advice when you are alone?
6. Would answering only `level` and `solo / with partner` feel like enough setup before a first session, or would something important be missing?
7. If the app later asked for today's time, wall/net access, wind, and pain/fatigue, would that feel helpful or annoying?
8. If an app gave you a short passing session with a few drills and one simple metric to log, what would make that feel useful versus fake?
9. If the app said this was `passing fundamentals for serve receive`, would that feel honest, too weak, or still too strong?
10. Would a one-tap `Good` / `Not Good` pass score tied to a visible target feel believable, or still too subjective?
11. If the app asked `How hard/intense was your session?` 10-30 minutes later, would you answer it or ignore it?
12. If you ended a session early, would `time`, `fatigue`, and `pain` feel like fair one-tap reason choices?
13. How much drill repetition feels productive to you before it starts to feel stale?
14. Would `progress / hold / deload` labels make intuitive sense after a session?
15. What would make you come back next week?
16. If you saw a weekly summary showing which sessions you did, your effort level, and one pass-success number, would that help you decide what to do next — or would it feel like homework?
17. Would queuing up your next 2-3 sessions for the week feel like useful structure or unnecessary planning?
18. Would you pay for something that reliably told you what to do and showed whether you were improving?

### Evidence to capture

- did the user understand the solo-first value instantly
- was passing fundamentals for serve receive a believable first focus
- did the short starter-session framing feel right
- what solo environment they actually have
- was metric logging acceptable
- were extra hard-filter toggles tolerated
- did the framing feel honest rather than fake
- was the binary `Good` standard clear enough to trust
- would delayed sRPE capture actually get answered
- did the early-end reason options feel fair and natural
- did purposeful drill repetition feel productive or stale
- did `progress / hold / deload` language make sense
- did the user ask for pair fallback immediately
- would a weekly receipt (compliance + load + skill) feel like enough to prompt next session
- would a next-N session queue feel useful vs. burdensome
- would they use it next week

## Wedge B: Coach Clipboard

### Best participant profile

- coaches beach athletes directly, remotely, or in small pods
- regularly assigns sessions and checks whether they were completed
- feels pain around follow-up, compliance visibility, or explaining progress clearly

### Core scenario

"You are coaching a beach athlete remotely. You want to assign a structured passing session, know whether they actually did it, see one or two quick signals about how it went, and decide whether the next session should progress, hold, or deload."

### Questions

1. How do you currently assign sessions or practices to athletes between your direct coaching time?
2. How do you know whether an athlete actually did the work you assigned?
3. When an athlete does report back, what signals matter most: did they show up, how hard was it, did a specific skill improve?
4. If you could assign a structured session from a drill library, and the athlete ran it on their phone and submitted a 60-second review with effort and one pass-quality number, would that be enough to adjust the next session?
5. What would make this clipboard loop clearly better than notes, spreadsheets, PDFs, or messaging threads?
6. Would you still find this valuable if it did not include video review, messaging, scheduling, roster management, or payments?
7. Would this save enough time or improve athlete compliance enough to justify paying for it?
8. Would you use it for the next real athlete or event cycle?

### Evidence to capture

- did the coach value compliance visibility, time saved, or planning quality most
- did they immediately ask for features beyond the clipboard loop (messaging, video, roster, payments)
- was the assign-complete-signal-adjust loop enough without heavy tooling
- did they want direct coach-to-client clipboard, centralized experts, or neither
- would they use it for the next real athlete or event cycle

## Closing questions for both wedges

1. If this existed and worked well, how often would you use it?
2. What is the one thing that would make it feel indispensable?
3. What would make you stop using it after the first week?
4. If this solved the problem well, what would feel like a fair monthly or one-time price?
