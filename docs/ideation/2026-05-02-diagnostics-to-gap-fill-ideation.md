# Ideation: Diagnostics To Concrete Gap Fills

## Grounding Context

Volleycraft already has generated diagnostics, triage groups, workload-envelope guidance, source-backed gap-card precedent, and a D47 proposal-admission ticket. The next challenge is not detecting observations; it is converting evidence into concrete drill inventory, programming/block-shape, workload metadata, generator-policy, or no-change outcomes, then reassessing whether the fill worked.

External grounding reinforces a closed loop: learning analytics must intervene, mastery learning uses correctives plus reassessment, curriculum mapping distinguishes promised/taught/assessed outcomes, and skills-gap tools turn diagnostics into targeted paths.

## Ranked Ideas

### 1. Diagnostic Gap Closure Ledger

**Description:** Make the primary artifact a ledger of known training gaps and closure states: missing drill/content depth, weak progression, workload metadata issue, block/programming-shape issue, generator artifact, accepted no-change, or filled/reassessed.

**Rationale:** It points diagnostics toward the real product goal: identify what the training system lacks and fill it concretely. It also absorbs the strongest remediation brief, improvement ledger, and admission queue ideas into one durable workflow.

**Downsides:** Needs careful scope so it does not become another broad dashboard before proving value on a small candidate set.

**Confidence:** 93%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Explored

### 2. Evidence Shopping Lists

**Description:** For each candidate gap, generate the missing evidence list needed to become fill-ready: source evidence, delta proposal, falsification condition, expected diagnostic movement, training-quality hypothesis, and no-action threshold.

**Rationale:** It turns vague investigation tasks into concrete research and proposal work without authorizing premature catalog edits.

**Downsides:** If too generic, it can duplicate the existing D47 admission ticket instead of helping fill gaps.

**Confidence:** 89%

**Complexity:** Low / Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 3. Auto-Reassessment Receipt

**Description:** Every accepted gap fill records before evidence, expected movement, actual movement after rerun, and whether training-quality/no-action thresholds were met.

**Rationale:** This closes the loop from diagnostic evidence to concrete change to reassessment, preventing clean diagnostics from being mistaken for improved training quality.

**Downsides:** Requires at least one concrete fill before it can prove its full value.

**Confidence:** 88%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 4. Drill Depth / Outcome Coverage Map

**Description:** Map player outcomes and programming roles to available drills, variants, levels, setups, duration bands, and assessment signals.

**Rationale:** It distinguishes real drill inventory gaps from metadata, block-shape, or generator artifacts.

**Downsides:** Higher modeling cost; could become too abstract unless driven by current diagnostics.

**Confidence:** 84%

**Complexity:** Medium / High

**Status:** Unexplored

### 5. Source-Backed Gap Card Drafts

**Description:** When diagnostics route to source-depth candidates, draft a gap-card shell with affected groups, candidate source needs, proposed fill shape, activation checks, and verification commands.

**Rationale:** This is the most direct bridge from missing drill/content depth to actual catalog improvement.

**Downsides:** Only helps content-depth gaps; workload/block/generator issues still need separate paths.

**Confidence:** 83%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 6. Counterfactual-First Drill Surgery

**Description:** Before editing drills or metadata, compare whether pressure would remain under relevant counterfactuals: no redistribution, cap change, min change, block split, or candidate content depth.

**Rationale:** It protects catalog work from being spent on generator artifacts or policy allowances.

**Downsides:** Can pull the team back into analysis mode unless tied to a concrete gap-closure ledger.

**Confidence:** 80%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 7. One Gap Per Cycle Queue

**Description:** Limit remediation to a small cadence, such as one diagnostic-backed gap fill per cycle, ranked by affected-cell impact, evidence readiness, and training value.

**Rationale:** It prevents the diagnostic backlog from becoming overwhelming and keeps founder-use work concrete.

**Downsides:** More process than product value unless paired with actual fill requirements.

**Confidence:** 77%

**Complexity:** Low

**Status:** Unexplored

## Continuation: Post-D01 Admission Ideas

After the D01 block-shape fill and redistribution handoff admission, the current D01 target is no longer a generic runtime issue. The receipt now says the remaining pressure is `insufficient_allocated_pressure`, with D47 routed to `cap_or_catalog_proposal_needed`. The next question is whether D01 needs a concrete cap proposal, a catalog/source-backed fill, an accepted no-change closure, or a D47 resume gate.

### 1. D01 Cap/Catalog Fork Admission Packet

**Description:** Create a proposal packet that forces the current D01 residual into one explicit fork: widen or accept the D01 cap, name a catalog/source-backed delta, close as no-change with a revisit trigger, or resume D47 because D01 cannot name a concrete delta.

**Rationale:** This is the shortest honest path toward actual catalog filling. It prevents another D01 runtime loop while still allowing a real drill-catalog gap to emerge if the evidence says D01 needs a longer beginner passing carrier.

**Downsides:** Adds one more admission artifact before catalog edits; the value depends on keeping the packet small and decision-forcing.

**Confidence:** 94%

**Complexity:** Low / Medium

**Status:** Explored

### 2. D01 Unsat Core / Smallest Missing Ingredient Receipt

**Description:** Extract the minimal reason each remaining D01 cell is impossible under current constraints: required focus/setup/level, allocated duration, D01 envelope, fatigue cap, and candidate pool.

**Rationale:** A cap/catalog decision is stronger if it can point to the smallest missing ingredient rather than aggregate affected-cell counts.

**Downsides:** Can drift into analysis tooling unless scoped as evidence inside the fork packet.

**Confidence:** 88%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 3. Beginner Passing Coverage Map

**Description:** Map beginner passing alternatives by setup, level, slot role, and duration capacity to determine whether the catalog has a real long-form beginner passing hole.

**Rationale:** This distinguishes "D01 cap too strict" from "the catalog lacks an honest longer beginner passing main-skill carrier."

**Downsides:** Higher modeling cost than a single D01 decision unless driven only by the current D01 affected cells.

**Confidence:** 86%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 4. No-Change Closure / Accepted Diagnostic Debt Ticket

**Description:** Make "do nothing for now" a first-class closure with rationale, owner, accepted blast radius, no-action threshold, and revisit trigger.

**Rationale:** If no honest cap or catalog delta exists, D01 should not remain open forever or keep blocking D47 by default.

**Downsides:** Needs careful wording so "accepted" does not become a way to ignore real training gaps.

**Confidence:** 82%

**Complexity:** Low

**Status:** Unexplored

### 5. Resume-D47 Gate If D01 Cannot Name Delta

**Description:** Add a rule that D47 resumes if the next D01 artifact cannot name a concrete cap delta, catalog/source-backed delta, or explicit no-change rationale.

**Rationale:** D01 partially validated the diagnostic-to-fill loop, but it should not monopolize the generated diagnostics workflow.

**Downsides:** Could resume D47 with a known D01 residual unless the gate records why that is acceptable.

**Confidence:** 80%

**Complexity:** Low

**Status:** Unexplored

### 6. Proposal Schema v1

**Description:** Define a reusable shape for cap, catalog, and source-backed proposals: target groups, concrete delta, evidence basis, expected diagnostic movement, training-quality hypothesis, no-action threshold, and U6 preview condition.

**Rationale:** A stable proposal schema would make future gap-fill loops cheaper and give U6 a concrete payload to consume.

**Downsides:** Better as part of the D01 fork packet than a standalone abstraction before one more concrete loop.

**Confidence:** 78%

**Complexity:** Medium

**Status:** Unexplored

### 7. D01 Pattern Learning Capture

**Description:** After D01 reaches a cap/catalog/no-change/D47-resume exit, capture the pattern in `docs/solutions/`: evidence-first diagnostics, proposal before behavior, partial validation, admission before handoff, and named exit.

**Rationale:** No durable learning exists yet for this workflow, and D01 is the first real diagnostic-to-fill loop.

**Downsides:** Premature until the next D01 exit decision lands.

**Confidence:** 74%

**Complexity:** Low

**Status:** Unexplored
