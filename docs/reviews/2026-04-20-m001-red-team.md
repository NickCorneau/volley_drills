# M001 Red-Team Review — D130 Founder-Use Mode and Tier 1 Plan

## Agent Quick Scan

- This note is a structured red-team of the post-D130 M001 plan, produced on 2026-04-20 by dispatching five persona reviewers (product-lens, scope-guardian, feasibility, adversarial, coherence) in parallel over the full doc set and spot-checking the code.
- Use this when re-evaluating Tier 1 scope, the D130 re-eval mechanism, or whether to ship the plan as written. Do not treat this as a final decision — the judgment calls at the bottom need the founder to choose before they propagate into the canonical plan docs.
- Authoritative for: the convergent red-team findings across personas, the five unambiguous fixes applied directly in this pass, and the open judgment calls.
- Not authoritative for: the plan itself. If this review and `docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-tier1-implementation.md` disagree after the founder chooses, the plan wins once updated; this review is the audit trail of the decision, not the new decision.

## Verdict (one sentence)

D130 was authorised to shrink the work and de-risk the wait; the plan that followed grew scope on content the partner may never run, deferred the trust surfaces the vision actually names, and installed a re-evaluation with no decision rule — so the single biggest risk of the current plan is ending up at 2026-07-20 with a larger catalog, a friendlier partner ledger, and no external evidence about whether any of it should have been built.

## The convergent picture across five personas

Every persona, working independently, found four of the same five problems. I'm ranking them by recurrence × severity.

### 1. Scope-ballooning paradox (product-lens, scope-guardian, adversarial, feasibility all agree)

D130's premise was "remove the D91 stranger-cohort gate so we can ship faster." The resulting Tier 1 is **larger** than the pre-D130 M001 scope:

- 26 → 45 drill records (73% growth)
- 2 new progression chains
- 3 new model-level fields (`SkillFocus='warmup'`, `openingBlock`, `sessionFocus`)
- 2 new user-visible `SetupScreen` toggles
- 3 new session-builder branches

Every persona reached the same conclusion independently: removing a validation gate should not have produced more authoring work. Scope-guardian proposed a **~60% cut**; product-lens proposed **flipping Tier 1 and Tier 2**; adversarial proposed a **hard cap on new drills per founder session logged**.

Evidence: `docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-tier1-implementation.md` Units 2, 3, 4 vs the milestone's Tier 1 summary in `docs/milestones/m001-solo-session-loop.md` L194-203.

### 2. The re-eval is decorative, not a gate (product-lens, feasibility, adversarial all agree)

D130 schedules a re-evaluation at 2026-07-20 with three options (friends-of-friends / reopen D91 / continue founder-only) but:

- **No decision rule.** Compare to D91 itself in `docs/research/d91-retention-gate-evidence.md` L91-99 which has explicit bands (0-1/5, 2/5, 3/5, 4-5/5). D130 has zero bands. Any outcome is narratable as "continue founder-only."
- **No watcher for the early triggers.** D130's three early triggers (5-in-45, 3-week silence, scope leak) are behaviorally well-specified but nothing — no calendar event, no cron, no code path, no memo ritual — actually watches them. `ExecutionLog` in Dexie has the raw data; there is no surface to read it until Tier 2 ships session history, and Tier 2 is gated on the very sessions that cannot be reviewed until Tier 2 ships.
- **Decision asymmetry.** All three D130 triggers point *down* (under-use). None point *up* (success-by-founder-only with no external exposure). "Continue founder-only" is a default-available choice with no burden of proof.
- **No external reader.** The re-eval memo is written by the founder, for the founder, with no required counter-signer. Pattern-name: perpetual founder-use mode disguised as a stage gate.

Evidence: `docs/decisions.md` D130 cell (L152); `docs/milestones/m001-solo-session-loop.md` L216.

### 3. The Pass · Serve · Set focus toggle contradicts D123 (product-lens, scope-guardian agree)

D123 ("recommend before you interrogate") is cited as authoritative in `docs/vision.md` P11 and `docs/milestones/m001-solo-session-loop.md` L100. Tier 1 Unit 4 adds a three-way focus decision the user makes *before* seeing a recommendation — the exact first-run move D123 argues against. The plan's framing as "in-place extension of SetupScreen, not a new screen" is semantically true and experientially false: a new first-run input on the activation flow is a first-run change regardless of which component it lives in.

Product-lens also flagged that this toggle is a **functional regression**: the recommendation-first first-run polish that Tier 2 promises is the surface the vision most emphasizes, and Tier 1 is adding friction to it before shipping the polish.

Evidence: `docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-tier1-implementation.md` L232-234; `docs/decisions.md` D123.

### 4. No evidence is being gathered between now and the re-eval (feasibility, adversarial, product-lens agree)

The D130 rationale explicitly requires "founder has run ≥N sessions weekly" as evidence. But:

- No session-history surface exists; Tier 2 has it.
- No weekly receipt exists; M002 has it.
- No founder dogfood log exists in the repo.
- No in-app "last opened N days ago" signal exists.

Plus, the founder has **not yet logged a weekly training pattern on the current app**. Tier 1 authors 19 new drill records while the founder has not exercised the existing 26 across 5 weekly sessions. Tier 1 acceptance §1 ("three sessions of different focuses in one week") tests that new content exists, not that it's worth returning to.

Adversarial scenario 6 names this pattern directly: **content authoring becomes displacement activity.** Authoring 3 focused weeks of new drills leaves less founder-energy for dogfooding them; at 2026-07-20 the founder has exercised maybe 12 of the 19 new records, with 7 never surfacing in a real session.

Evidence: `docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-tier1-implementation.md` L338-348 acceptance bar; no `docs/research/founder-use-ledger.md` exists.

### 5. Partner walkthrough cannot carry the evidential weight the plan gives it (adversarial, feasibility, product-lens agree)

The walkthrough script is well-authored and epistemically honest about what it is *not* (`docs/research/partner-walkthrough-script.md` L32-46, L217-222). But the plan treats it as a Tier 1 gating input ("no open P0 content bugs"), which creates two problems:

- **P0 gate is structurally unsatisfiable during content growth.** A thoughtful first-time user finds ≥1 P0 nearly every time. Fix-cycle → re-walkthrough → new P0s is self-sustaining. No timebox on Tier 1 stabilisation.
- **Friend-of-founder politeness bias.** The one behavioural return-question (Q9, "would you open it again?") is buried ninth and phrased hypothetically. Task C is done with the founder physically present. The walkthrough can produce "looks great" when the real behaviour would be silent non-return.

Adversarial's fix: move Q9 to Q1, rephrase as a measurable behavioural follow-up, and add "partner opens app unprompted ≥1x in 30 days post-walkthrough" to Tier 1 acceptance.

- **`last_updated` drift.** Milestone and roadmap are at `2026-04-20-b`; D130 lives in decisions at `-c`. All three should be at `-c` since D130 is what those docs describe.

### Scope-guardian: Unit 6 is already shipped

The scope-guardian inspected `app/src/data/archetypes.ts` and found the four invariants are already present verbatim at lines 12-52. **Unit 6 of the plan is a no-op.** (Verified directly.) The plan is planning work that exists on disk. This is a coherence issue masquerading as a scope issue — it suggests the plan was written from the spec rather than from the code.

### Feasibility: schema and type realities

- **`SessionDraft.context` is defined in `app/src/db/types.ts` (`SetupContext`), not in `app/src/types/session.ts` (`SessionContext`).** The plan references the wrong file. Both types exist and have divergent wind enum values (`'breezy'|'windy'` vs `'light'|'strong'`). Tier 1 Unit 2 must edit `SetupContext`; the plan doesn't mention this split.
- **Replay integrity is actually safe.** `DraftBlock` and `SessionPlanBlock` snapshot drill copy at build time (`app/src/db/types.ts:3-12, 45-56`). Unit 5 vocabulary rewrites will not retroactively change historical session records. One pre-existing concern goes away.
- **Unit 2 and Unit 4 must add their new context fields as optional.** Otherwise legacy plans read with `SessionPlan.context?: SetupContext` will break. The plan does not say "optional" explicitly; flag to implementer.
- **`m001Candidate: false` on `d24` is already set.** Unit 3's plan to "mark `d22`–`d24` non-candidate" is partially redundant.

### Adversarial: the forward-trigger asymmetry

Adversarial scenario 1 (the perpetual-founder-use trap) is the single most important finding in the whole review. The argument is structural and I missed it in my own pre-review scan:

> All three D130 early triggers detect **under-use** (too few sessions, long silence, covert scope leak). **None detect over-success-by-founder-only.** The re-eval's option (c) ("continue founder-only") has no burden of proof beyond founder discretion. The plan treats "the founder kept using it alone" as evidence of success rather than a risk state.

Fix: add a **fourth D130 trigger pointing up**:
> "(d) Tier 1 + Tier 2 have shipped AND ≥10 founder sessions are logged AND partner walkthrough returned no open P0s — re-eval MUST resume `D91` preparation."

Remove option (c) from the default menu. Make "continue founder-only" an opt-in requiring a written falsifiable justification co-signed by a named non-founder reader.

### Product-lens: BAB-literacy drift

Tier 1 authors `Sideout`, `Tomahawk`, `Pull Dig`, `Joust`, `Triangle Setting`, `3 Before 5 Format` vocabulary (`docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-tier1-implementation.md:259-283`). A first-run amateur does not have this vocabulary. By optimizing the product around a BAB-literate user (the founder), the drill content is drifting the product away from the vision's "amateur beach player" target into a power-user curriculum tool. The glossary header partially mitigates this but does not replace the fact that beginners see drill copy before they see the glossary.

## What I would change (ranked by impact on getting to the vision)

The ranking below is my synthesis, not any single persona's. Items 1-3 are structural; 4-8 are more mechanical.

### 1. Pre-register an adversarial memo before any Tier 1 authoring begins

Adversarial's "single change most likely to prevent the most failure modes" is correct. The deliverable is a one-page `docs/plans/2026-04-20-m001-adversarial-memo.md` containing:

1. **Three falsification conditions** for "this works for me," behavioural and pre-committed:
   - *Solo-first:* ≥40% of logged sessions must be solo across 90 days; solo-set sessions must total ≥3. Below either bar, solo-first is falsified and Tier 2 re-sequences pair-first.
   - *Trust-surface deferral:* if the founder plans any session outside the app during 90 days (Notes, paper, another app), Tier 2 trust surfaces (See-why + session history) move forward out of Tier 2.
   - *Partner walkthrough:* if partner does not open the app unprompted ≥1x in 30 days after walkthrough delivery, that is a P0 content/trust issue regardless of ledger contents.
2. **A default decision rule for 2026-07-20** that is asymmetric against inertia:
   - *"If Tier 2 acceptance has passed AND combined founder+partner sessions >12, the default decision is option (a) friends-of-friends cohort. Continuing founder-only (option c) requires a written falsifiable justification co-signed by the partner or a named non-friend reviewer."*
3. **A weekly 3-minute D130 trigger review**, pasted into a recurring Monday calendar event, with one-line log appended to the memo.
4. **Authoring budget tied to dogfood:**
   - *"≤10 of the 19 new drill records ship before the 5th real founder session on the post-Tier-1a library. The remaining records are authored only in response to a concrete session-builder gap observed in a specific session."*

This memo installs an adversary in the room at every future discretion point. It is the single highest-leverage addition across the whole review set.

### How the two reviews converged

The second review produced mechanical findings: specific broken code paths, specific progression dead-ends, a specific session-duration math error, and a specific P11 UI violation. Those yielded a targeted list of code-level fixes.

The two sets are **complementary, not conflicting.** The structural rescope (cut content, install adversarial memo, rewrite walkthrough for behavioural signal) creates the conditions under which the mechanical fixes become unnecessary in Tier 1a (no toggle → no formification; no progression links → no dead-ends; no opening-block in Tier 1a → no duration overflow; one-shot vocabulary sweep → inline definitions are the natural output). The combined rewrite ships a Tier 1a that is both smaller than the five-persona review alone would have produced (because the mechanical review pruned further) and safer than either review alone would have produced (because the architectural prerequisites for Tier 1b and Tier 1c are now documented in advance).
