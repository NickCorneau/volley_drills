# Manual test notes — “Player 3” (reluctant amateur)

**Context:** I don’t really follow volleyball apps. A friend asked me to click through Volleycraft on my laptop so they’d have “real person” feedback. I did one full path: blow off an old session → new setup → safety → run (skipped ahead a lot) → review → complete → peek at Settings.

**How it reads (tone):** Everything sounds careful and a bit “coach in your pocket.” That’s probably good if you want training vibes, but if you’re not into that, lines like *Chosen because: …* and the long drill paragraphs feel like homework. I’d still use it if the timer kept me honest.

**Does the flow make sense?** Mostly yes. Big picture is: pick how you’re set up outside → answer a few health-ish questions → timed blocks with a break/transition between → rate how it went. I didn’t get lost.

---

## What worked

- **Resume vs discard:** When I opened the app it yelled that a session was in progress (block 4 of 4, paused a minute ago). Annoying but fair — I’d forgotten. Discard took an extra confirm; I didn’t hate that. The line about progress going to history but not being resumable was clear enough.
- **Today’s setup:** Solo / pair, net, wall, time, wind — that matches how I’d describe practice to someone at the court. “Build session” is an obvious button.
- **Before we start:** The “sharp pain” question felt grown-up without being scary. I like that it says normal soreness is fine. “Heat & safety tips” being there but not forced is reasonable.
- **During the workout:** Pause + Next + optional coaching cues is simple. The transition screen (“up next”, time on the next block) bridges the jump between drills without me having to remember the plan.
- **Quick review:** Picking how hard it was (0–10) is fast. Telling me I had to rate effort before Submit lit up was obvious from the helper text.
- **Wrap-up:** “Today’s verdict” and the little recap table (session type, drills 4/4, effort) felt like a natural stop. “Back to home” is clear.
- **Settings:** Super small — export JSON and a privacy reminder. I didn’t need more for a test.

---

## What was fuzzy or mildly annoying

- **“0 days since last train” wording:** I get what you mean after reading the hint (trained today → easier start), but the first time I saw *0 days* I had to think for a second. If I’m in a hurry I might tap wrong and not notice.
- **Same word “No” twice on safety:** There are two different questions and both have Yes/No chips. Not wrong, but my brain had to check I was answering the second question, not clicking the same No again by muscle memory.
- **Drill text density:** On the run screen there’s a lot of lines for some blocks (numbered steps in one blob). Courtside sun + sweat, I’d want the top line to be the *one* thing to do right now, with the rest collapsed unless I expand.
- **“Good passes” on review:** Counting Good vs Total with rules about “target zone” is fine for people who track stats; as a casual tester I’d probably leave both at zero or tap “Couldn’t capture reps” every time. Not broken — just maybe more serious than I am.
- **Timer anxiety:** “Keep the phone unlocked so the block-end beep can fire” (or similar) is practical but also makes me think I’m managing the app instead of the workout. Minor.

---

## Logic / trust check

- Order of screens felt right: **setup → safety → work → review → done.** Nothing jumped backward without me choosing Back.
- Letting me **Next** through blocks quickly (for testing) didn’t crash anything; the app still ended on review with “Complete” even though real time was basically a minute — if that’s intentional for dev, cool; if not, maybe real users would find that weird in the recap.

---

## Bottom line (if my friend forces me to sum up)

It’s more thoughtful than I expected from a volleyball thing I didn’t ask for. The flow is logical. The copy is a little earnest for someone who’s just trying to get touches in, but I’d still rather this than a blank notes app and a stopwatch.

— *Anonymous “Player 3,” April 21, 2026*
