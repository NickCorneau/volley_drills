# Research sources — primary drill-book and framework PDFs

Primary-source PDFs that back the synthesis in active research notes (e.g.
`docs/research/beach-training-resources.md`,
`docs/research/ltd3-development-matrix-synthesis.md`) and related specs
(`docs/specs/m001-session-assembly.md`, `docs/specs/m001-adaptation-rules.md`).
Treat these as read-only references; synthesize into the markdown research
docs rather than linking users to them directly.

Secondary PDFs that were evaluated and not promoted live in
`reference-only/` alongside their own README.

## Drill books

- `FIVB_Beachvolley_Drill-Book_final.pdf` — FIVB Beach Volleyball Drill-book.
  Canonical source for the drill-as-family model (objective, equipment,
  participants, teaching points, variations) and for participant-minimum claims
  (e.g. Diamond Passing 2×4 serves, passing drills that assume "athlete +
  coach").
- `2024_Better_at_Beach_Volleyball_Drill_Book.pdf` — 2024 Better at Beach
  Volleyball drill book. Community-level drill catalog; useful for cross-checking
  drill families and beginner progressions that the FIVB book assumes a coach
  for.

## Volleyball Canada LTD / Development Matrix (framework)

- `LTD3.0_240111.pdf` — **A Roadmap for Volleyball in Canada — Long Term
  Development 3.0** (Volleyball Canada, 2021; version 2024-01-11). Canonical
  source for stage definitions (Active Start → FUNdamentals → Learn to Train →
  Train to Train → Learn to Compete → Train to Compete → Learn/Train to Win →
  Active for Life, with Competitive-for-Life and Fit-for-Life sub-phases), for
  the explicit guidance that adults who take up volleyball in their 20s–40s+
  "will benefit from developing the skills and the capacities outlined in this
  document for the Train to Train stage", and for the indoor / beach / sitting
  discipline-integration matrix.
- `VDM_Nov_5_2025_EN.pdf` — **Volleyball Development Matrix** (Volleyball
  Canada, 2025-11-05). Canonical source for the three-pillar matrix (Person /
  Athlete / Player), the 4S + 3S athlete model (Speed, Strength, Suppleness,
  Stamina + Sustenance, Sleep, Structural Tolerance), the stage-specific
  Active-for-Life sleep table, the canonical 0–10 pain scale with the "level 2
  for 3 consecutive days → medical professional" escalation rule, the
  Structural Tolerance load progression table, the Adaptation Principle
  definition, and the training-vocabulary glossary.
  - **Supersedes** `VDM_May_8_2023_EN.pdf`. New claims should cite the
    November 2025 edition; the older file is kept below for citation
    traceability only.
- `VDM_May_8_2023_EN.pdf` — **Superseded** earlier edition of the Volleyball
  Development Matrix (Volleyball Canada, 2023-05-08). Do not cite for new
  claims; use `VDM_Nov_5_2025_EN.pdf` instead. Retained because existing notes
  (e.g. `docs/research/beach-training-resources.md`) link to it directly.

## Volleyball Canada LTD one-pagers (stage summaries)

Lightweight stage-specific summaries that back the "what does this stage
actually look like" claims in the synthesis. Each maps to a multi-page section
in `LTD3.0_240111.pdf`.

- `L2T_1_pager_22_11_25.pdf` — Learn to Train (girls ~8, boys ~9 to onset of
  adolescent growth spurt). 70% practice / 30% compete; 2–3 practices/week.
- `T2T_1_pager_22_11_25.pdf` — Train to Train (period of the adolescent growth
  spurt). 60/40 practice/compete. **This is the stage whose skill and capacity
  content LTD 3.0 recommends adult beginners work through.**
- `L2C_1_pager_22_11_25.pdf` — Learn to Compete. 40/60 practice/compete.
- `T2C_1_pager_22_11_25.pdf` — Train to Compete. 40/60 practice/compete.

## Volleyball Canada Person Pillar (coaching content)

- `Person_Pillar_Guidebook_for_Coaches_EN.pdf` — **Person Pillar Guidebook for
  Coaches** (Volleyball Canada). Operationalizes the Person pillar from the
  VDM into coach-facing indicators of desired / undesired state, reinforcing
  comments, and reflection questions for each dimension (Common Needs →
  Connection → Care → Courage). Canonical template for any Volleycraft
  copy that tries to coach the *person* (reflection prompts, post-session
  self-talk, courtside reframes) rather than just the athlete or the player.
